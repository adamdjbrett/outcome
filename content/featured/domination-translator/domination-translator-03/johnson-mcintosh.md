## The Marshall Trilogy: Johnson v. McIntosh, 21 U.S. (8 Wheat.) 543 (1823) - Domination Translator Series - Part 3

The 1823 landmark Johnson v. McIntosh case established the Doctrine of Discovery as U.S. law, denying Native nations' property rights.

### About This Article

This article is Part 03 of the comprehensive Domination Translator Series, which traces how the Doctrine of Discovery has shaped American law and policy from the founding of the republic to the present day.

### Key Concepts

The Domination Translator Series examines:
- How medieval principles of Christian supremacy became U.S. law
- The role of Supreme Court decisions in perpetuating discovery doctrine
- Contemporary impacts on Native nations and sovereignty
- Legal arguments and counterarguments regarding indigenous rights
